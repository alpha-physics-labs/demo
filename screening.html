Why this shape of prototype, and why heat matters

01

Flat plate first

The clean, controlled coupon isolates material and layup from helmet curvature, and its data is far more shareable than a fielded design.

02

Heat is a real failure mode

Ceramics stay stable to thousands of degrees, but a UHMWPE backing softens near 80 °C. A plate that stops a round cold can fail hot. The tool flags it.

03

Validated on your data

Nothing leaves the partner environment. They run the model against their own flat plate results and see the accuracy for themselves.

On heat. Two effects. At impact, intense local heating softens materials for an instant, and hard ceramics ride it out far better than polymers. In the field, the environment sets a baseline temperature, and the polymer backing is the weak link long before the ceramic is. A screening tool that scores only the cold case would miss the case that actually fails.

What the flat plate data needs. For each shot: material, areal density, layup and backing, threat and velocity, test temperature, and the result, which is V50, stop or perforation, residual velocity, and back face deformation. A few hundred rows in that form let us build and honestly validate a first model.
